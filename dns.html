Domain Name System

From Wikipedia:

The Domain Name System (DNS) is a hierarchical decentralized naming system for computers, services, or other resources connected to the Internet or a private network. It associates various information with domain names assigned to each of the participating entities. Most prominently, it translates more readily memorized domain names to the numerical IP addresses needed for locating and identifying computer services and devices with the underlying network protocols.

What does this mean? It translates yourdomain.com into your web page!

Domain Name Providers

A domain name provider supplies a person or business with a domain name which can point to their web pages or services.

They will also provide you with a public IP address for your website and may even provide domain names.

Some hosts may also provide easy design interfaces and web management panels to make it easy to change the content of your website. A more "hands-off" approach can be taken as well if the host deals with updating your website software to make things easier.

Top Level Domains

A top level domain is the .com, .org, or .xyz (or tons of others) you can purchase for your website address.
